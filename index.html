Top 8 Reason to NOT "Love Thy Neighbor"

By Cooper Bower

You’ve heard it a million times: “Love thy neighbor.” Sounds wholesome, right? Well… not so fast. According to Sigmund Freud also well known as the father of psychoanalysis and low-key humanity’s biggest skeptic loving your neighbor might actually be one of the worst ideas anybody could have came up with. In Civilization and Its Discontents, Freud basically drops the mic on human relationships and explains how our neighbors aren't always the path to peace. In fact, they're often the reason we're miserable. From backstabbing friends to the emotional chaos that is romance, Freud is here to say that minding your own business is the real key to happiness. So buckle up for 8 brutally honest reasons why “love thy neighbor” might be the last piece of advice you should follow if you're trying to survive modern civilization with your sanity intact.

Number 8

Man is a Crual Being

Freud doesn’t sugarcoat it, he argues that humans are inherently selfish, driven by primal instincts that are more about survival than sainthood. At our core, he says, “man is not a gentle creature” (Civilization, p. 68), and that wild streak makes it seriously hard to love the people around us without things spiraling into conflict, competition, or straight-up chaos.
