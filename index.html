The Constitution

Article 1

  • Section 1

    All legislative powers are given to congress which is made up of two chambers, the house and the senate.

  • Section 2

    The house of representatives is the lower chamber, and the senate is the upper house of congress.The number of members in the house for each state is done by population and is recounted every 10 years. Members of the house must be at least 25 years of age, be a US resident for at least 7 years, and live in the state they represent. In voting each person counts as 1, except for people in service. Their vote counts as three fifths.

  • Section 3

    Each state will have 2 senators. They will have a 6 year term. They must be at least 30 years old and a US citizen for at least 9 years. The president of the senate is the Vice president of the US.They also have the role of impeachment and with a two thirds majority they can impeach and remove the president from office.

  • Section 4

    States have authority on deciding on how votes should be conducted, this includes place, time, and manner. Congress has the power to oversee this process and can alter it for a fair election of members of the house. Congress also has to meet at least once a year so that congress remains in session at regular times.

  • Section 5

    In order for the house or the senate to conduct official business more than half of the members must be present at that time. Neither one of the houses can adjourn for more than three days without the consent of the other house, this ensures that one house isn't preventing legislative activities. Each house has the authority to compel the attendance of absent members, ensuring that members fulfill their duties. Both houses create their own rules and must keep a journal of their findings. These journals must be published to the public unless they need to be secret. Members of both houses can expel members of their respective house with a two thirds majority.

  • Section 6

    Members of congress will receive compensation for their service from the US treasury. The amount is determined by law. While performing their duty, members of congress are immune to civil arrest, this ensures that they will not be hindered in performing their duties. During section members of congress have speech and debate immunity so that they can express their view freely. Members of congress cannot also hold a position in the executive branch.

  • Section 7

    All bills for raising taxes or increasing revenue are to originate in the house of representatives. For a bill to become a law it needs to have a majority in both houses, the law can be proposed by either house. Once it's a law it's sent to the president to approve or veto it, if he vetoes it congress can override it with a two thirds majority in both houses. If the president doesn’t do anything within ten days it becomes a law. However if congress adjourns during that period then it is not a law, this is called a “pocket veto”

  • Section 8

    Congress has the authority to levy and collect taxes, duties, imposts, and excises to raise revenue for the federal government. Congress can also borrow money on US credit. Congress regulates relations with foreign nations. Congress can establish rules on becoming a citizen and law surrounding bankruptcy. Only congress can coin money and establish the properties around it like weight, price, etc.congress can establish post offices and post office roads to ensure communication with the country.congress grants patents for copyright to promote invitation and protect intellectual property. Congress can also raise and support an army. Funding for the army must be reauthorized every two years. Congress can call forth a militia. Congress can make laws if they are necessary and proper for carrying out its enumerated powers.

  • Section 9

    Congress cannot the writ of habeas corpus unless in cases of invasion, rebellion or when public safety requires it. Congress cannot pass laws of attainder. They can also not criminalize ex-post-facto laws. Direct taxes are portioned based on individual states' populations. Exports are also not taxed.Congress cannot grant titles of nobility in order to prevent an aristocracy. Treaties require a two thirds majority to pass and money bills must originate in the house. The slave trade also ends in 1808.

  • Section 10

    States cannot enter treaties, alliances, or confederations with foreign nations. States cannot coin money as that is done by congress.states also cannot pass laws that impair contracts. Ike congress states do not have the power to give titles of nobility. States also don't have the power to levy taxes on any imports and exports. States cannot engage in war unless they are in dire need to fight back in the event of invasion. States can also not engage in war like activity with other states unless in the case of immediate threat. Only with the consent of congress can they.

Article 2

  • Section 1

    The president must be natural born to the US and be of at least 35 years of age. They also have been a resident of the US for at least 14 years. The president has a four year term and a maximum of two terms. The president is not directly elated by the people but is elected by the electoral college. The members of this are decided by the amount of representation in congress that state has. Congress is in charge of counting the votes of the electoral college. If there is no majority vote the house votes out of the top three candidates. Before the president takes office they must take an oath saying that they will protect, preserve and defend the constitution.

  • Section 2

    The president is the commander and chief of the US military. The president has the power to write and sign treaties with the consent of two thirds of congress. The president can also appoint judges, ambassadors, and other officers.The president can fill vacant spots in the senate when they are in recess. However, that ends at the end of the senate next seccion. The president can grant pardons for crimes, except if the president is impeached by congress.

  • Section 3

    The president must also give congress updates on the state of the union. The president is expected to recommend laws to congress that are necessary to the well being of the country. The president can also call congress into session. He can also adjourn them when he sees necessary.

  • Section 4

    The president and vice president as well as all officers can be impeached for high crimes and misdemeanors by congress. If this official is impeached the senate can vote to have him removed from office. If there is a two thirds majority then that person is removed from office. After removal from office the officer can have further penalties.

Article 3

  • Section 1

    The judiciary branch is made up by the Supreme Court. Congress has the power to make smaller courts. The federal power of the US is to be vested in these courts.

  • Section 2

    Federal courts handle cases about federal law, the constitution, ambassadors, public ministers, disputes between states and cases where the US is a party. Cases filed directly with the supreme court are the only ones handled by the supreme court. This includes cases filed by lower courts. A criminal must have a trial by jury. The jury must be from the state where the crime was committed.

  • Section 3

    In order to be convicted of treason, there must be either two witnesses, or a confession in a public court.

Article 4

  • Section 1

    Each state must give “full faith and credit” to the public records and judicial proceedings of every other state. In short, they must recognize and respect the legal decisions and official document of the other states.

  • Section 2

    Every citizen displite what state they are originally from has the same right and privileges as the people who live in that state. So people from another state cannot discriminate against people from another state. Also if a person frees to another state to escape justice that state they feed to must return them to face trial.

  • Section 3

    Congress has the power to admit new states into the union. Congress has the power to establish the conditions dn regulations for admission into the union. When a new state is admitted into the union it has the same equal power as the rest.The federal government has power over territories and other properties that belong to the US. This also includes the power to make rules and regulations for those territories.

  • Section 4

    Every state in the union must have a republican form of government. The federal government also must protect the states against invasion and domestic violence or reoccuring uprising. In this situation the states can request assistance from the federal government.

Bill of Rights

  • Amendment 1

    Citizens are granted the freedom of religion, speech, press, assembly, and to petition the government. Religion means that they can be a part of any religion they want. Speech means that they can not be criminalized for anything they say. Press means that the press can publish any news they want. Assembly means that people are allowed to have peaceful protest without being criminalized, however it must be peaceful. And Petition means that they are allowed to express their grievances and see redress from the government, this includes policies or laws.

  • Amendment 2

    A militia is important for a state to have, this means that citizens have the right to bear arms. This does not mean they need to be a part of the militia. The government should not touch this rule as much as possible.

  • Amendment 3

    The government cannot make homeowners provide for soldiers. In the case of war quartering soldiers should be done with regulations established by the government, this ensures that quartering is done in a lawful manner.

  • Amendment 4

    The government cannot search private homes, that person is entitled to their belongings including papers, people, and other property. To conduct a search the government must get a warrant and thi s required that there is reasonable belief that there is a crime committed. The warrant can be bypassed in cases of emergencies. Any evidence collected in violation of the fourth amendment is disregarded. This is so that there is less incentive to search homes.

  • Amendment 5

    In cases of serious crimes the individuals have the right to a grand jury. Also a person cannot be tried for the same crime twice, this is called double jeopardy. A person can also not incriminate themself, they have the right to remain silent during questioning. A person is also guaranteed due process of law; they cannot be deprived of life, liberty, or property without legal procedures. The government cannot take private or public property without just compensation; for instance, if a highway needs to be built but a house is on the way, the owner of the house will get compensation.

  • Amendment 6

    Defendants in a trial have the right to a speedy and timely trial. Trials are to be held publically, and are open to the public and press. The jury of a trial must be an impartial one that will not be biased. The defendant in a trial must be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation. Defendants also have the right to confront and cross-examine witnesses who testify against them in court. The defendants can also compel witnesses to testify on their behalf as long as they use legal processes. The defendant in a trial also has the right to a lawyer. If they cannot afford one, they will be provided one.

  • Amendment 7

    In civil cases parties have the right to have their case heard and decided by a jury of their peers. The right to a jury trial is preserved when the value exceeds 20 dollars, however this is not adjusted for inflation. Once facial evidence is brought up it cannot be reexamined or changed.

  • Amendment 8

    The amount to bail for someone's release cannot be unreasonably high. This also includes excessive fines not being allowed as a punishment for criminal offenses. A person cannot get cruel or unusual punishment in criminal cases. This means torture, inhumane treatment, or something that is disproportionately harsh to the crime.

  • Amendment 9

    There are more rights to an individual than just the right outlined in the bill of rights and the constitution. This doesn’t mean that rights not outlined are denied.

  • Amendment 10

    Any power not enumerated by the constitution is not prohibited to the states. States also have individual sovereignty and that they have a wide range of powers that allow them to keep their sovereignty.

  • Amendment 11

    States cannot be sued in federal courts but individuals. The federal government can hear cases in which a citizen of one state sues another state. States also retain sovereignty. States are protected from certain types of legal action in federal courts.
